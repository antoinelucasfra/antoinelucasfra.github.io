Email LinkedIn GitHub Twitter CV

I trained as an agronomy engineer, specialized in applied statistics, and somehow ended up building machine learning systems for Chanel’s fragrance R&D. The path made sense at each step, even if it doesn’t look obvious on paper.

For the past 4+ years I’ve been embedded in R&D and clinical teams at L’Oréal, Sanofi, Abolis (microbiome biotech), and now Chanel Parfums Beauté — always at the intersection of data and experimental science.

Currently Data Scientist & ML Engineer at Chanel Parfums Beauté R&D, on mission via Astek / IT&M Stats. Building ML and computer vision tools for fragrance and cosmetics research.

What I do

Statistical modeling & analysis

From design of experiments and mixed models to biostatistics in GxP environments. I cover the full analytical chain — from raw assay data to interpretation for regulatory submissions.

R Shiny applications

I build internal tools that scientists actually want to use. Dashboards for QC monitoring, SPC, pilot manufacturing, clinical data management. The measure of success is whether the app disappears — people just use it.

Machine learning & computer vision

Predictive models in fragrance formulation, image classification pipelines, NLP for raw material descriptions. Production-grade tools that go into daily research workflows.

Reproducible data science

Everything in Git, documented, runnable in 6 months with renv::restore() or uv sync. I’m opinionated about this because I’ve seen what happens when you’re not.
